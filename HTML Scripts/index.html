About NASA

NASA, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, was established on July 29, 1958, by the National Aeronautics and Space Act. The formation was a direct response to the Soviet Union's successful launch of Sputnik, the world's first artificial satellite. NASA's early missions were focused on breaking new ground in aeronautics and getting humans into space. The agency achieved significant milestones, including the Apollo Moon-landing missions, the Space Shuttle program, and the construction of the International Space Station in collaboration with other nations. NASA has also been responsible for launching various unmanned missions to explore the Earth, Mars, and beyond. Today, NASA continues to push the boundaries of science and technology as it aims for new missions, including sending humans to Mars.
